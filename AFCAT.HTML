AFCAT (Air Force Common Admission Test)

NOTE: Registration for AFCAT will start soon on the official website of Airforce. For Registration, the website link is given below.

Air Force Common Admission Test is conducted by Air Force Selection Board for recruitment of ground staff and flying branches of the Indian Air Force (IAF). Qualified applicants in the test are mandated to register for the five-day Air Force Selection Board process. Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT) is conducted in two stages. The first stage consists of a written test and the second stage involves a testing process. Call letters to report to Dehradun, Mysuru, Gandhinagar, Varanasi, or Guwahati (any one of the selection boards) are sent to qualified candidates of the testing process.


Eligibility:


  • Citizenship: Candidates must be Indian citizens.
  • Age Limit: Candidates must be between 20 and 24 years of age.
  • Educational Qualification: Candidates must have completed BE or BTech Degree.

Examination Process:


The Indian Air Force recruits the candidates through the Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT). The AFCAT is held twice a year. The candidates will have to appear for the stage 1 (written test) and the selected candidates will be called for the next stages in the AFSB centers which are located in Dehradun, Mysuru, Varanasi, Gandhinagar, and Guwahati. AFSB Testing will include 2 stages. The medical examination will be the last stage of the selection process for AFCAT Recruitment 2024 for the posts of Flying Branch and Ground Duty (Technical and non-Technical). Finally, a merit list will be curated for the qualified candidates. Candidates can go through the article for the detailed AFCAT Selection Process 2023.


Written Examination:


The online computer-based AFCAT Exam Pattern is divided into four sections: General Awareness, Numerical Ability and Reasoning, Verbal Ability in English, and Military Aptitude Test. Further information on the subject is provided below:

  1. It includes 100 multiple-choice questions worth 300 Marks.
  2. It consists of 50 multiple-choice questions worth 150 Marks.
  3. Regardless of the posts chosen, all the questions are objective.
  4. The question paper is provided in English.


Services Selection Board Interview:


The Indian Air Force compiles a list of candidates who meet the AFCAT cut off in the online written Test. Those who apply for NCC Special Entry and Meteorology Branch will be called directly for AFSB testing. The AFSB Interview is divided into three stages.
Stage I of the AFSB Interview
A screening test is the first stage of an AFSB interview. It is held on the first day of the candidate's arrival at the AFSB center and consists of two tests. They are as follows:

  • Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) Examination
  • Picture Perception and Discussion Test (PPDT)
